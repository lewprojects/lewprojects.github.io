My Data Odyssey: From Teaching to Tech

In 2006, I got a degree that meant a lot to me but wasn't too marketable: Music Composition. Saddled with a large amount of student loan debt and eager to get out my parents basement, I traveled to South Korea to teach ESL and pay down my loans.

During my time there, I found it was difficult to motivate the students, so I started to incorporate A/V materials into my lessons. Unbeknownst to me, this was the start of my data journey. I began extracting, cleaning, analyzing and visualizing data in order to improve my classes. Of course, I didn't know that at the time; I was just doing what I could to help my students.

I returned to the US in 2018 and was hired to build a band program at a local charter school. During COVID, I was tasked with putting on marching show, with 20 students, no instruments or uniforms and a tiny budget of 10,000 dollars. Since there were so many things to organize, I took some project management courses on LinkedIn Learning. Armed with a Gantt chart laying out my tasks, milestones and project timeline, I set to work doing everything I could to put on a show. I succeeded.

After seeing my work organizing the band program, the admin at the school asked me to organize a computer science program. I was extremely hesitant because I had no interest in coding whatsoever.
