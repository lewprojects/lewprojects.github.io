My Data Odyssey: From Teaching to Tech

In 2006, I got a degree that meant a lot to me but wasn't too marketable: Music Composition. Saddled with a large amount of student loan debt and eager to get out of my parents basement, I traveled to South Korea to teach ESL and pay down my loans.

During my time there, I found it was difficult to motivate the students, so I started to incorporate A/V materials into my lessons. Unbeknownst to me, this was the start of my data journey. I began extracting, cleaning, analyzing and visualizing data in order to improve my classes. Of course, I didn't know that at the time; I was just doing what I could to help my students.

I returned to the US in 2018 and was hired to build a band program at a local charter school. During COVID, I was tasked with putting on a marching show, with 20 students, no instruments or uniforms and a tiny budget of 10,000 dollars. Since there were so many things to organize, I took some project management courses on LinkedIn Learning. Armed with a Gantt chart I made in Microsoft Project laying out my tasks, milestones and project timeline, I set to work doing everything I could to put on a show. I succeeded.

One parent labled us "The cutest marching band in Texas."

After seeing my work organizing the band program, the admin at the school asked me to organize a computer science program. I was extremely hesitant because I had no interest in coding whatsoever. Isn't that just a bunch of math, ones and zeroes and hexidecimals? I was extremely apprehensive about. My admin recommended using a free program code.org that had a pre-made easy-to-follow curriculum. Now I was just apprehensive.

But as it turned out, I really enjoyed learning to code and learning about the software development process with the students. Coding was much more about problem solving than math. And code.org's curriculum was less focused on teaching facts and more focused on developing and implementing a team based, project centered computer science curriculum. Students learned about internet safety, the software development process, AI, and machine learning. Students completed individual and group projects building websites, games and app prototypes using basic HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

JavaScript game made by one of my students.

Because I enjoyed the the project management aspect of my job much more than the teaching aspect (and since teaching wasn't paying the bills), I decided I would try to see if I could get hired as a project manager. I got certified as a Scrum Master in 2022 as well as a PMP certification. Unfortunately, the job market in 2023 was rough. While job searching it became obvious that I needed some kind of specialization to break into project management. I decided to try and specialize in data analysis.

In 2023, I started the Google Data Analytics certification and was shocked to find that everything I had been doing in Korea to create AV curriculum was the exact process data analysts also used. Additionally, I found that the coding instruction I had done more than prepared me to work with SQL and Python. I completed my certificate in early 2024 and have been working with data ever since.
